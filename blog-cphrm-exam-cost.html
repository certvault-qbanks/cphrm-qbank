CPHRM Exam Cost in 2026: Fees, Prep Materials, and Total Investment

10 min read • April 2026

← Back to all articles

If you're planning to sit for the CPHRM exam, one of the first questions you'll have is: how much is this going to cost? The answer depends on your ASHRM membership status, which prep resources you choose, and whether you pass on your first attempt. This guide breaks down every cost involved so you can budget effectively and avoid surprises.

CPHRM Exam Application Fee

The exam itself is administered by the AHA Certification Center through PSI testing centers. The fee depends on whether you're a member of ASHRM or another AHA Professional Membership Group (PMG).

CategoryExam Fee
ASHRM / AHA PMG Member$275
Non-Member$425

This is a one-time application fee that covers the exam sitting. If you fail and need to retake within 90 days, you can submit a shortened reapplication — but you'll need to pay the full fee again. After 90 days, a completely new application and full fee are required.

Pro tip: If you're not already an ASHRM member, do the math. An individual ASHRM membership plus the member exam rate can save you money compared to the non-member rate, and you get access to ASHRM's professional resources, webinars, and discounts on study materials. The savings on the exam fee alone often offset a significant portion of the membership cost.

The exam fee itself ($275–$425) is fixed, so your prep investment is where you have flexibility. The real question is what combination of materials gives you the highest probability of passing on your first attempt — because failing and retaking costs another $275–$425 plus additional study time.

Is the CPHRM Worth the Investment?

With only a 64% pass rate, the CPHRM exam is genuinely challenging. But the certification carries significant career weight. It's widely considered the premier credential for healthcare risk management professionals, and many leadership-level risk management positions either require or strongly prefer CPHRM-certified candidates.

Healthcare risk managers with the CPHRM designation typically command higher salaries than their non-certified peers. While exact figures vary by geography, organization type, and experience level, the certification signals a validated level of expertise that employers actively seek.

The ROI calculation is straightforward: even a modest salary increase of $5,000–$10,000 per year — well within the range that certified professionals report — pays back your entire certification investment within the first year.

How to Get Your Employer to Pay

Many healthcare organizations cover certification costs as part of their professional development benefits. If your employer doesn't have a formal policy, consider making the case directly. Emphasize that the CPHRM certification aligns with organizational goals around patient safety, regulatory compliance, and risk reduction. Many risk managers successfully get their employer to cover the exam fee, study materials, and even travel costs for in-person prep courses.

Additionally, if you qualify for VA benefits, the cost of the CPHRM exam may be reimbursable through the VA's licensing and certification test fee reimbursement program (VA Form 22-0803).

Maximizing Your Prep Budget

Given the 64% pass rate, the goal isn't to spend the least amount possible — it's to spend enough to pass on your first attempt. A retake costs more in fees, time, and stress than investing in thorough preparation upfront.

The most cost-effective approach for most candidates combines a content review resource (like the ASHRM guide or a comprehensive study guide) with an interactive question bank that provides scenario-based practice and detailed explanations. Key Takeaways

The total cost of earning your CPHRM certification ranges from roughly $450 on the low end to $2,200+ for the premium path. The exam fee ($275 for members, $425 for non-members) is your baseline, and prep materials add $50–$1,000+ depending on your approach. The most important factor isn't how much you spend — it's whether your study approach gives you realistic, scenario-based practice that mirrors what the actual exam tests.
